China #2

2021 PIB: ¥105.4 trillion 2021 GDP per capita: ¥76,211

China is the world's most populous country with over 1.4 billion people. It is located in East Asia and has a diverse landscape that includes the Himalayan mountains, the Gobi desert, and the Yangtze River. China has a rich history and culture, and its contributions to philosophy, literature, and the arts are renowned throughout the world.

China has the world's second-largest economy by nominal GDP, and is a leading manufacturing and trading power. Its economy is characterized by a mix of state-owned enterprises and private businesses. China is also a major player in the tech industry and has a rapidly growing middle class.

In addition to its economic power, China is also a key player in global politics. It is a permanent member of the United Nations Security Council and is a member of various other international organizations. China's military is the largest in the world, and it participates in peacekeeping missions and other international activities. The country's culture and values, including Confucianism and Taoism, have had a significant impact on global culture.
