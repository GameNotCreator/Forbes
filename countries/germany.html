Germany #4

2021 GDP: $4.2 trillion 2021 GDP per capita : $50,348

Germany is a country located in Central and Western Europe, with a population of over 83 million people. It is known for its rich history and culture, as well as its contributions to science, philosophy, and the arts.

Germany is the largest economy in Europe and the fourth-largest in the world by nominal GDP. It has a highly skilled and productive workforce and is home to many world-renowned companies, particularly in the automotive and engineering industries. The country is also a leader in renewable energy, with a goal of achieving 65% renewable energy by 2030.

Germany is a federal parliamentary republic with a president as its head of state and a chancellor as its head of government. It is a member of the European Union and plays a key role in shaping the political and economic landscape of the region.

Germany is known for its strong commitment to social welfare and environmental protection, and is widely regarded as a leader in these areas. The country is also home to many world-class universities and research institutions, making it a hub of innovation and scientific progress.
